## 1. Princeton Review

**Ad Library URL:** facebook.com/ads/library — The Princeton Review
**Active Ads:** ~44 (21 unique texts extracted)
**Ad Run Dates:** Majority started Jan–Mar 2026

### Hooks Used

| Hook Type | Example | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Score guarantee (refund) | "Get a 1400+ or 150+ point improvement — or your money back." | **Very High** — appears in ~60% of ads |
| Authority / longevity | "40+ years of experience" | High — ~40% of ads |
| Direct question | "Looking for the best SAT and ACT prep?" | Medium |
| Social proof (ratings) | "4.5/5 stars from Trustpilot reviews" | Medium |
| Flexibility hook | "Flexible schedules designed for busy teens" | Medium |
| In-person differentiation | "In-person SAT + ACT courses near you" | Medium |
| Test coverage breadth | "SAT + ACT prep — because your teen shouldn't have to choose" | Medium |

### CTAs Used

| CTA Button | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sign Up | **Dominant** (~70%) | Standard across most ads |
| Learn More | ~25% | Secondary option |
| Shop Now | Rare | Seen on a few variants |

### Emotional Angles

| Angle | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Parental anxiety relief | Target parents worried about their teen's future | "Give your teen every advantage" |
| Risk reversal | Money-back guarantee removes financial fear | "1400+ or your money back" |
| Trust / proven track record | Decades of experience = safety | "40+ years helping students succeed" |
| Convenience for busy families | Flexible scheduling reduces logistics stress | "Flexible schedules designed for busy teens" |
| Competitive edge | Positioning as the serious choice | "The best SAT and ACT prep" |

### Specific Numbers / Claims

| Claim | Context |
|---|---|
| 1400+ SAT score guarantee | Core value prop — or money back |
| 150+ point improvement guarantee | Alternative framing of same guarantee |
| 40+ years of experience | Longevity / authority claim |
| 4.5/5 stars | Trustpilot social proof |
| SAT + ACT coverage | Dual-test positioning (vs. single-test competitors) |
| In-person courses | Physical location differentiation |

### Key Patterns & Takeaways

- **Guarantee-led strategy:** Princeton Review's entire ad library revolves around the score guarantee. It's their single strongest differentiator and appears in the majority of ads.
- **Parent-focused targeting:** Language consistently addresses parents ("your teen," "busy teens," "flexible schedules"), not students directly.
- **In-person as differentiator:** Multiple ads emphasize physical classroom experience, positioning against digital-only competitors.
- **Dual-test coverage:** SAT + ACT combo positioning is repeated, suggesting they see this as a key consideration-stage objection handler.
- **Low creative variety:** Despite ~44 active ads, the messaging is highly repetitive — most are variations of the same 4-5 core messages with different creative assets.

## 2. Kaplan

**Ad Library URL:** facebook.com/ads/library — Kaplan Test Prep
**Active Ads:** ~57 (51 unique texts extracted)
**Ad Run Dates:** Majority started Dec 2025–Mar 2026

### Hooks Used

| Hook Type | Example | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Official ACT partner | "Official partner of the ACT" | **Very High** — anchor of ~50% of ads |
| Third-party endorsement | "Test Prep Insight rated Kaplan #1 for live online ACT prep" | High — ~30% of ads |
| Price anchoring | "Flexible plans starting at $30/month" | High |
| College admissions bundling | "SAT/ACT prep + college consulting — all in one package" | High |
| All-in-one package | "Kaplan's College Advantage package" | Medium |
| Question hook | "Looking for the best ACT prep?" | Medium |
| Urgency / seasonal | "Start today — limited enrollment" | Low |

### CTAs Used

| CTA Button | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sign Up | **Dominant** (~55%) | Standard CTA |
| Shop Now | ~25% | Notable — more e-commerce framing than competitors |
| Learn More | ~20% | Secondary |

### Emotional Angles

| Angle | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Authority / official partnership | ACT official partner = institutional trust | "The only prep backed by the makers of the ACT" |
| Third-party validation | External rating removes "just trust us" burden | "#1 for live online ACT prep — Test Prep Insight" |
| Affordability / accessibility | $30/month removes price objection | "Flexible plans starting at $30/month" |
| Comprehensive solution | One-stop-shop for college readiness | "SAT/ACT prep + college consulting" |
| Future investment | Framing prep as investment in college future | "Get an edge for college admissions" |

### Specific Numbers / Claims

| Claim | Context |
|---|---|
| Official ACT partner | Institutional endorsement — unique to Kaplan |
| #1 for live online ACT prep | Test Prep Insight third-party rating |
| $30/month | Entry-level price anchor |
| College Advantage package | Bundled prep + consulting product |
| SAT + ACT + college consulting | Three-in-one value proposition |

### Key Patterns & Takeaways

- **ACT-heavy positioning:** Unlike Princeton Review (SAT + ACT), Kaplan leads heavily with ACT, leveraging their official partnership.
- **Third-party proof over self-claims:** "Test Prep Insight" quote appears across many ads — they clearly test external validation vs. self-reported claims.
- **"Shop Now" CTA variant:** Kaplan is the only competitor using e-commerce-style CTAs, suggesting they're optimizing for direct purchase intent rather than lead gen.
- **Price transparency:** $30/month is prominently featured — no other competitor in this set leads with price in their ads.
- **College consulting upsell:** The "College Advantage" bundle positions Kaplan beyond just test prep into full college readiness, expanding the value prop.
- **High creative volume but moderate variety:** 57 ads with many duplicates/minor variations. Core messages rotate around 5-6 themes.

## 3. Khan Academy

**Ad Library URL:** facebook.com/ads/library — Khan Academy
**Active Ads:** ~8
**Ad Run Dates:** Feb 23–Mar 6, 2026

### Hooks Used

| Hook Type | Example | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| AI-powered tools | "AI-powered teaching assistant" / "Khanmigo" | **High** — ~50% of ads |
| Free positioning | "Khan Academy is 100% free for teachers!" | **Very High** — appears in every ad |
| Teacher-focused | "Here's the ONE thing you need for your classroom" | High |
| Re-engagement | "You don't need to start over — your account is ready" | Medium |
| Practice-first philosophy | "Your AI tools work best with real practice" | Medium |
| Trust / reliability | "Reliable resources. Trusted by teachers." | Medium |

### CTAs Used

| CTA Button | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sign Up | ~50% | Used on Khanmigo/teacher-focused ads |
| Learn More | ~37% | Used on practice-focused ads |
| Sign up (lowercase) | ~13% | Minor variant |

### Emotional Angles

| Angle | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Teacher empowerment | Reduce prep time, increase student time | "Spend less time on prep and more time with students" |
| Free = no risk | Zero cost removes all barriers | "100% free for teachers" |
| Trust / legitimacy | Not random internet content | "Legit sources, not just random internet stuff" |
| Ease of adoption | Low effort to start | "Easy class setup" / "Pick up where you left off" |
| AI as assistant (not replacement) | AI coaches, doesn't give answers | "Use AI to guide, explain, and coach (not give answers)" |

### Specific Numbers / Claims

| Claim | Context |
|---|---|
| 100% free | Core positioning — in every single ad |
| Millions of teachers | Social proof scale |
| Khanmigo | AI tool brand name |
| Standards-aligned practice | Curriculum compliance claim |

### Key Patterns & Takeaways

- **Completely different market:** Khan Academy targets TEACHERS, not parents or students. This is a B2B2C play — get teachers to adopt, students follow.
- **Free as the ultimate weapon:** Every single ad mentions "100% free for teachers." This is their version of Princeton Review's guarantee — an objection-killer that appears everywhere.
- **AI positioning is nuanced:** They're careful to position AI as a coaching tool that doesn't give answers — addressing teacher concerns about AI enabling cheating.
- **Influencer/ambassador strategy:** One ad uses "magicallearningadventure" — a teacher influencer partnership. This is the only competitor in this set using creator content for B2B targeting.
- **Tiny ad volume:** Only 8 active ads vs. 44-57 for Princeton Review/Kaplan. Suggests either: (a) highly focused spend, (b) reliance on organic/earned media, or (c) different paid strategy (Google > Meta).
- **Re-engagement ads:** "You don't need to start over" targets lapsed users — sophisticated lifecycle marketing that no other competitor is doing on Meta.

## 4. Chegg

**Ad Library URL:** facebook.com/ads/library — Chegg
**Active Ads:** ~25 (18 unique texts extracted)
**Ad Run Dates:** Jan 20–Mar 6, 2026

### Hooks Used

| Hook Type | Example | Frequency |
|---|---|---|

| Step-by-step solutions | "Step-by-step breakdowns and study tools" | High |
| Student UGC / creator | Influencer-style "#ad #chegg #cheggcreator" posts | High (~30% of ads) |
| Personalization | "More personalized help customized to your classes" | Medium |
| Peer social proof | "Hot take: Students are giving their study hacks that actually work" | Medium |
| Metaphor / playful | "Searching for homework help can feel like you're lost on a deserted island" | Medium |
| AI tools | "Standard AI does it all. Chegg's AI tools do what matters" | Low |

### CTAs Used

| CTA Button | Frequency | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sign Up | **Dominant** (~70%) | Standard across most ads |
| Subscribe | ~15% | Used on influencer/creator content |
| Sign up (lowercase) | ~15% | Minor variant |

### Emotional Angles

| Angle | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| "We get it" empathy | Acknowledging the struggle of being stuck | "When you're stuck on homework, Chegg finds the missing piece" |
| Community / togetherness | "We're in it together" / "In it together" | Recurring tagline across multiple ads |
| Personal support | Not generic — customized to YOU | "Personalized help customized to your classes" |
| Overcoming overwhelm | Turning confusion into clarity | "No more fumbling around in the dark" |
| Real student voices | UGC from actual students validates the tool | Creator content from Michell, Brooke Neal, Sierra, etc. |

### Specific Numbers / Claims

| Claim | Context |
|---|---|
| 20% off first month | Promo code: IGAmbassador20 (creator-specific) |
| Step-by-step solutions | Core product differentiator |
| Chegg Study + Chegg Study Pack | Product tier naming |
| "Standard AI does it all. Chegg's AI tools do what matters" | AI differentiation claim |

### Key Patterns & Takeaways

- **Student-direct targeting:** Chegg is the ONLY competitor targeting students directly (not parents, not teachers). Language is casual, relatable, peer-level.
- **Heavy creator/influencer strategy:** ~30% of ads are UGC-style creator content (#ad #cheggcreator). This is significantly more than any other competitor.
- **Empathy-first hooks:** Most ads lead with acknowledging the student's struggle, then positioning Chegg as the solution. Very different from Princeton Review/Kaplan's achievement-focused messaging.
- **"In it together" brand campaign:** This tagline appears across multiple ads as a unifying brand message — community-focused positioning.
- **Weak on specifics:** Unlike Princeton Review (1400+ guarantee) or Kaplan ($30/month, official ACT partner), Chegg's ads are vague on measurable outcomes. No score guarantees, no specific results.
- **AI as quiet differentiator:** Only one ad mentions AI explicitly, suggesting Chegg is testing AI positioning but hasn't committed to it as a primary message yet.
- **Promotional pricing in creator content:** 20% off codes appear only in influencer posts, not in brand-direct ads — smart segmentation of discount messaging.

### Top Insights for Varsity Tutors Ad Strategy

1. **Own "human tutoring" on Meta.** Every competitor has ceded this ground to tools, AI, courses, or self-serve. VT is the only brand that can credibly claim "a real expert who adapts to your student." This should be the lead message in 50%+ of ads.

2. **Replace "Learn More" with "Sign Up" or stronger CTAs.** Every winning competitor uses "Sign Up" as their dominant CTA. VT's over-reliance on "Learn More" (scored as a bad CTA per Nerdy's framework) puts them at a disadvantage in the feed.

3. **Steal Chegg's empathy hook + add PR's specificity.** The best-performing ad formula across competitors would be: empathize with the struggle (Chegg's approach) → offer a specific, measurable promise (PR's approach) → deliver it through a human (VT's unique advantage). No competitor currently combines all three.

4. **Test price anchoring.** Kaplan's $30/month messaging is smart — it reframes a large purchase into a manageable monthly cost. VT should test similar anchoring ("Expert tutoring from $X/session" or "Your first session is free").

5. **Double down on named student outcomes.** VT's Jessica and Sarah ads already outperform generic messaging. Combine this with Princeton Review-style specificity: "Jessica raised her SAT score 200 points with her Varsity Tutors expert."

6. **Target the teacher channel.** Khan Academy is the only competitor targeting teachers — and with only 8 ads. Teachers are recommendation engines for tutoring. A small VT campaign targeting educators ("Recommend expert tutoring to your struggling students") could open a new acquisition channel.
